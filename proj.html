The US national debt

A wise man once said “Beware of little expenses. A small leak will sink a great ship.” The US national debt is the total amount of outstanding borrowed money the government has accumulated over time, for every year the US spends more money than it earns, the result is a budget deficit. Every year the federal government runs a deficit, and the national debt grows as a result of the increasing amount of money borrowed. Without borrowing money, however, the government could not fund basic programs and public services critical to Americans’ health, welfare, and security.
